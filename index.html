Act 5

Scene 1

Romeo is sitting in Mantua when Balthasar comes and tells him that Juliet is "dead". Romeo decides to visit Juliet's grave and kill himself so he doesn't have to live without her. He calls Apocethary who sells him an illegal poison that would kill him fast.

Scene 2

Friar John returns to Friar Lawrence to tell him he could not tell Romeo of the plan due to a disease. Friar Lawrence heads to the Capulets' tomb to tell Romeo personally.

Scene 3

Paris comes to the entrance of Capulets' tomb to pay his respects for Juliet and heads inside and sends his servant away. Romeo also arrives with Balthasar. Balthasar leaves and Romeo opens the tomb. Paris recognises Romeo's voice and decides to apprehend Romeo. Romeo and Paris fight and Paris' page runs out to get the watch, Paris falls. Paris asks Romeo to lay him with Juliet as he dies, which he does. Romeo then proceeds to drink the poison and dies, falling over Juliet. Friar Lawrence arrives to find Balthasar outside, they enter the tomb to find Paris and Romeo dead. Juliet wakes up and finds out what happens. She kissees Romeo to try and consume any leftover poison on his lips but hears noise from outside and grabs Romeo's dagger and kills herself. the watch arrives and rounds up Balthasar and Friar Lawrence. Prince Escales, Capulet and Lady Capulet arrive seeing what happened. Montague enters, explaining that his wife had died earlier and sees everything. Prince asks Friar Lawrence what happened and Friar Lawrence explains everything about how Romeo and Juliet were married and the plan to sneak Juliet out of Verona. Capulet and Montague make peace with eachother and the books ends.
